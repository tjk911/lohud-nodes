Dan Futterman

Dan Futterman has been celebrated for his work both in front of and behind the camera.

Back in 1997, he won a Screen Actors Guild Award for Outstanding Performance by a Cast for "The Birdcage."

His first Oscar nod came for 2005's "Capote." Futterman was nominated for Best Adapted Screenplay, but that award went to "Brokeback Mountain."

This year, Futterman is nominated along with E. Max for writing "Foxcatcher."

  • There was once a chance you'd be looking at two lohud Oscar winners in this scene from 2007's "A Mighty Heart." In 2013 it was reported Angelina Jolie was looking to buy the 11-acre Petra Island in Lake Mahopac. The deal never came through, but she has other local connections: Jolie's father, Jon Voight, was born and raised in Yonkers. In "A Mighty Heart," Futterman plays Daniel Pearl, an American reporter who was captured and killed by terrorists in 2002.
  • Futterman played Val Goldman in the 1996 comedy "The Birdcage," a character forced to hide the fact that he has two gay fathers, played by Robin Williams and Nathan Lane. Futterman had only done three films before being cast in the Mike Nichols-directed film. Following Nichols' November 2014 death, Futterman told "The Hollywood Reporter" that the "Graduate" director would often have to monitor Williams' scenes from another room because he couldn't contain his giggling.
  • Futterman teamed up once again with his Mamaroneck High School buddy Bennett Miller for 2014's "Foxcatcher." Miller directs Steve Carell, Channing Tatum and Mark Ruffalo in the film from a script by Futterman and E. Max Frye. Carell, Ruffalo, Miller and Futterman are all nominated for their work on the film.

His first nod came in 2011, when he was nominated for directing "The Fighter."

For his two follow-up films, 2013's "Silver Linings Playbook" and 2014's "American Hustle," Russell was nominated for both writing and directing.

Michael O'Keefe

O'Keefe has only been nominated for one Academy Award, but considering it was for just his second feature film, that's quite a feat.

He was up for Best Supporting Actor for the 1979 film "The Great Santini," which also earned a Best Actor nod for Robert Duvall.

O'Keefe can be seen in the 2007 thriller "Michael Clayton," which was up for seven Oscars, including Best Picture.

O'Keefe was also just nominated for a Screen Actors Guild Award along with the rest of the cast of "Homeland."

O'Keefe appeared in the 2007 thriller "Michael Clayton," which brought home a supporting actress statue for Tilda Swinton, as well as nominations for best actor, director, writer and film.

Kevin Dillon

Kevin Dillon has never been up for an Oscar, but he's gotten plenty of love for his television work.

For his role as Johnny "Drama" Chase on "Entourage," Dillon was up for Emmys in 2007, 2008 and 2008, as well as the 2008 Golden Globe.

Oliver Stone's "Platoon," in which he played Bunny, took home four Oscars, including Best Picture and Best Director, and was nominated for another four.

  • Dillon, seen here on the far right, will be reprising his role as the dim-witted, hot-tempered Johnny "Drama" Chase when HBO releases the "Entourage" movie, which reunites the crew after a four year hiatus. Having played the character for eight seasons, Dillon will surely be screaming "Victory!" when the film releases in June.
  • Despite his character's name being Bunny, Dillon was far from soft and cuddly in Oliver Stone's "Platoon." His character shows a lust for violence, savagely beating a Viatnamese villager to death in one of the films more disturbing scenes. The 1987 film was nominated for eight Academy Awards, bringing home four, including Best Picture and Best Director.
  • Dillon broke on through to the other side in the 1991 Jim Morrison biopic "The Doors." He played John Densmore, the drummer of the titular band. In this scene from the Olive Stone film, Dillon, seen on the far right, is venturing on a drug-fueled excursion into California's Death Valley.

Matt Dillon

Matt Dillon got a Best Supporting Actor nod at the 78th Annual Academy Awards for playing Officer John Ryan in Paul Haggis' "Crash."

Though Dillon didn't win, the film brought home awards for editing, Best Screenplay and Best Picture.

  • Dillon's breakout role came as Dallas Winston in Francis Ford Coppola's 1983 film, "The Outsiders." Helping to spark the Brat Pack craze of the ‘80s, the film also starred Emilio Estevez, Rob Lowe and Ralph Macchio. On Coppola's days off from filming, he reportedly spent his time adapting the script to "Rumble Fish," which came out later that same year and also starred Dillon.
  • Matt Dillon's only Oscar nod came for his role as John Ryan, a racist police officer working in a diverse L.A. neighborhood, in the 2004 film "Crash." The film boasts other Oscar darlings, including Sandra Bullock, who won Best Actress for "The Blind Side" and was nominated for "Gravity" and Don Cheadle, who was up for Best Actor for 2005's "Hotel Rwanda." While Dillon lost out to George Clooney, "Crash" still brought home the awards for Best Film, Best Original Screenplay and Best Achievement in Editing.
  • Proving he also has comedy chops, Matt Dillon starred as an obsessed private investigator in the 1998 comedy hit "There's Something About Mary." If he seems especially convincing lusting after Cameron Diaz's title character in the film, that's because the two were dating in real life during filming, but broke up after production wrapped. Somewhat unsurprisingly, "There's Something About Mary" wasn't up for any Oscars, but it was nominated for a Golden Globe for Best Motion Picture - Comedy or Musical.
